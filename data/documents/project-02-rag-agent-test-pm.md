# Project Management and Scrum Master Guide

## Introduction

Project Management is the discipline of planning, organizing, and managing resources to successfully complete project goals within scope, time, and budget constraints.

A Scrum Master is a facilitator and coach who helps Agile teams follow Scrum practices and continuously improve their performance.

---

# Project Management

## Key Objectives

* Deliver projects on time.
* Stay within budget.
* Meet quality standards.
* Satisfy stakeholder expectations.
* Manage risks effectively.

## Project Lifecycle

### Initiation

* Define project goals.
* Identify stakeholders.
* Create a project charter.

### Planning

* Define project scope.
* Create schedules and timelines.
* Estimate costs and resources.
* Identify risks.

### Execution

* Complete project tasks.
* Coordinate team activities.
* Manage communications.

### Monitoring and Controlling

* Track project progress.
* Manage changes.
* Monitor risks and issues.
* Report status to stakeholders.

### Closure

* Deliver final product.
* Obtain approvals.
* Conduct lessons learned sessions.

---

# Agile Methodology

Agile is an iterative project management approach focused on:

* Customer collaboration
* Continuous delivery
* Adaptability
* Incremental improvements

## Agile Values

* Individuals and interactions over processes and tools
* Working software over comprehensive documentation
* Customer collaboration over contract negotiation
* Responding to change over following a plan

---

# Scrum Framework

Scrum is a popular Agile framework used to manage product development.

## Scrum Roles

### Product Owner

Responsible for:

* Managing the Product Backlog
* Prioritizing work
* Maximizing business value

### Scrum Master

Responsible for:

* Facilitating Scrum events
* Removing impediments
* Coaching the team
* Promoting Agile principles

### Development Team

Responsible for:

* Delivering product increments
* Self-organizing work
* Maintaining quality standards

---

# Scrum Events

## Sprint

A fixed time period (typically 1–4 weeks) during which work is completed.

## Sprint Planning

The team selects backlog items and defines the Sprint Goal.

## Daily Scrum

A 15-minute daily meeting to discuss:

* Progress
* Plans
* Blockers

## Sprint Review

Demonstration of completed work to stakeholders.

## Sprint Retrospective

Discussion of improvements for future sprints.

---

# Scrum Artifacts

## Product Backlog

Prioritized list of requirements and features.

## Sprint Backlog

Selected work items for the current sprint.

## Increment

Potentially releasable product delivered at the end of a sprint.

---

# Scrum Master Responsibilities

## Facilitation

* Organize Scrum ceremonies.
* Encourage collaboration.

## Coaching

* Guide teams in Agile practices.
* Support self-management.

## Impediment Removal

* Resolve blockers.
* Escalate issues when necessary.

## Continuous Improvement

* Facilitate retrospectives.
* Promote process optimization.

## Stakeholder Support

* Improve communication.
* Increase transparency.

---

# Common Agile Metrics

## Velocity

Amount of work completed in a sprint.

## Burndown Chart

Tracks remaining work over time.

## Lead Time

Time from request to delivery.

## Cycle Time

Time required to complete a work item.

---

# Risk Management

Common project risks include:

* Scope creep
* Budget overruns
* Resource shortages
* Technical challenges
* Stakeholder conflicts

Mitigation strategies:

* Regular monitoring
* Clear communication
* Risk assessments
* Contingency planning

---

# Best Practices

* Maintain a prioritized backlog.
* Hold regular retrospectives.
* Keep meetings focused and time-boxed.
* Encourage team ownership.
* Deliver value incrementally.
* Continuously improve processes.

---

# Summary

Project Managers focus on planning, execution, budget, timelines, and stakeholder management.

Scrum Masters focus on enabling Agile teams, facilitating Scrum practices, removing obstacles, and fostering continuous improvement.
